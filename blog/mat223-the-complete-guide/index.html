MAT223: The complete guide

As a student at the University of Toronto(UofT) who takes a lot of math, statistics, and computer science courses, I always wished there were guides published online by students who took the class. So instead of waiting until I graduate until one comes out, I decided to create my own for all future students who take MAT223: Linear Algebra. I hope this proves to be helpful in your studies and if there is anything that could be done to make it better please don’t be afraid to contact me.

In this guide, I will do my best to try to organize the content, explain topics, and provide relevant links if I feel I can’t adequately convey a topic. As well I will highlight definitions that are expected to be known for the test. The first article will be about some of the foundational knowledge but contains definitions so don’t skip it.

FAQ:

Why are you working on this?

Good question! As a student, I have to study for this course and sink a lot of time into it. On top of that, one of the most effective ways to study is to teach a concept to another person. The second reason is I would like to help my peers better understand the course material. I've found that I waste a lot of time trying to understand vague concepts from lecture and tutorial. Collectively we all waste even more time, and if I can save someone who struggles like me a bit of time, it's worth it. The final reason is it adds content to my website which I hear employers like when you can show that you can build something. Overall I have to study so why not help others and build my resume in one go.
