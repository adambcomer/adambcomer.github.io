MAT223: The complete guide

As a student at the University of Toronto(UofT) who takes a lot of math, statistics, and computer science courses, I always wished there were guides published online by students who took the course. So instead of waiting until I graduate until one comes out, I decided to create my own for all future students who take MAT223: Linear Algebra. This guide will be based on what is expected of students in the Fall of 2018. I hope this proves to be helpful in your studies and if there is anything that could be done to make it better please don’t be afraid to contact me.

In this guide, I will do my best to try to organize the content, explain topics, and provide relevant links if I feel I can’t fully convey a topic. I will highlight definitions that are expected to be known on test day. The first article will be about the foundational knowledge but contains definitions and I don’t recommend skipping it.

This is a work in progress

Why are you working on this?

Good question! As a student, I have to study for this course already and one of the most effective ways to study is explaining concepts to other people. The second reason is that I would like to help my fellow students better understand the course material. Spending time piecing together definitions for different curriculums at different universities can be an uphill battle. The third reason I’m working on this is it adds content to my website which I hear employers like when you can show the work you are doing. Overall I have to study so why not help and build my resume in one go.
